About

I am a full stack developer with an unusual background. Originally studying physics as an undergraduate at Imperial College London. I then moved toward teaching with a teaching degree from University College London, before deciding to go back to physics for a PhD at the University of British Columbia.

While there, I developed a new mesocopic entropy measurement technique. More importantly, during that time, I spent a huge amount of time coding, going well beyond the typical data analysis of a scientist. Towards the end of my PhD I became more serious about software development and learnt as much as I could from SQL database management to sassy styling of web apps.
